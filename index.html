about me

A little bit about me

Work experience: 10 years My childhood was full of interaction with animals such as cats and dogs. We brought home stray animals and took care of them. Some of them stayed with us and others we helped find new families. My last dog, named Dev, was a German Shepherd, but he died of cancer, which was a big shock to our family. After that I decided not to get a new pet and concentrated entirely on my work and helping other animals and their owners.

I helped friends and acquaintances with their puppies and adult dogs, and I was interested in learning about the psychology of animals, understanding their aggression, fears, overexcitement and other behavioral issues. I studied manuals, attended various courses and lectures, both in Russia and in England, and put my knowledge into practice. This led to people recommending me to each other, and my visits to dogs in various cities started to take more and more time.

I decided to give up my main job in London, where I was doing shows, to devote myself to helping people and animals. This is how my own business started. I am constantly learning about different methods of working with dogs and putting them into practice, observing different breeds. I also study nutrition and consult with veterinarians and other trainers. I am very happy that my life is associated with animals, and knowing that I am contributing to society and helping dogs lead a dignified and happy life without fear or aggression with their owners makes me feel confident that my life has meaning.

my services

Training

I am ready to help you become a true friend and reliable partner for your pet. I believe that socialising with your dog should be a pleasure for both you and your pet. My classes are conducted in a friendly and emotionally supportive atmosphere so that you will find it easy and enjoyable to work with your dog.

book

Overexposure

I understand the importance of providing your dog with quality care and nurturing. That's why this service includes: building your dog's routine, learning how to communicate properly with other dogs, teaching restraint and boundaries, teaching how to walk properly on a lead. Reactions, fears and aggression are corrected as needed. I also often take my dogs out in the summer to crowded places, to the sea, and to transportation so that they can adapt to different conditions.

Behavioural correction

Often dog owners are faced with behavioural problems - how to toilet train, how to beg, how to deal with aggression or fears in a dog, so our approach is based on a deep understanding of dog behaviour and psychology. I offer individual programmes that are tailored to the specific needs and characteristics of each dog.
